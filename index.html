Bari

From Bari it is easy to reach various tourist destinations in Puglia. Alberobello with its trulli is just over 50 km away; the charming Polignano a Mare, with its houses overlooking the sea and its beautiful beach, is just 35-40 km away; and Trani, Andria, the white villages of the Itria Valley, the caves of Castellana, are all destinations that can be reached within a day. Not far from Bari, in nearby Basilicata, is Matera, the European Capital of Culture 2019.

Malaga

Malaga is the fifth largest city in Spain, and one of the most important tourist destinations in the world. The city of Malaga has a very special charm that any visitor can identify in a short walk. The culture, traditions, gastronomy, an enviable climate and the hospitality of its people serve to consolidate the image of the "city of paradise", the name used by the famous Spanish poet Vicente Aleixandre, Nobel Prize for poetry and literature, in reference to the city.

Santorini

Blue domes, white houses and spectacular sunsets: this is Santorini, the southernmost and mildest of the Cyclades able to enchant travelers from all over the world for its unique landscape. Santorini explodes with charm. A volcano embraced by the Aegean, this island phenomenon in the Cyclades offers its visitors a mythical experience. Santorini - also known as Thera in Greek - is the island immortalized by poets and painters, thanks to its famous light , the multicolored cliffs and postcard sunsets.

Italy

There are not a lot of countries in the world which offer quite as much as Italy. This gorgeous country is home to some of the best wines, delicious foods, and most beautiful sights in the world. Italian lands fascinate and broaden the horizons, and are worth the visit at least once in a lifetime. Every traveler will be able to find something unique in this stunning country, something that will make them come back again and again.

Spain

Spain really does have it all, from incredible landscapes and beaches, to world-famous cuisine and rich history – not to mention fantastic museums and striking architecture. Spain may be renowned for its beaches and sunny climes, but it is a country of diverse landscapes. Its mountains are majestic, from the northern Pyrenees and the Picos de Europa to the Sierra Nevada in the south. Spain will never disappoint its visitors.

Greece

Greece is home to marvellous monuments and UNESCO heritage sites. Great beaches, wonderful food, friendly people, history and culture are just a taste of what this amazing Mediterranean country can offer. Greece has over 200 inhabited islands, and each one is a reason to visit Greece!
